From access to impact

Licenses don't transform firms. Systems do.

Over the past decade, regulated industries bought AI the way they bought software: a license, a login, a rollout memo. The tools were capable. The impact rarely arrived — because generic AI doesn't know your precedents, your margin thresholds, your house style, or the difference between a clause your partners will sign and one they never would.

OTC Hedge was built on the other approach. We embed with your teams, learn how the work actually moves, and build AI systems around that reality — from enablement, through custom agents, to production-grade platforms your firm operates and owns. We've processed more than 500,000 contracts this way, managing business-as-usual while the transformation ships.

The result isn't a pilot deck. It's a working system — and a team that knows how to run it.

Operating principles

Three commitments, every engagement.

01 /

Custom build — never configuration theater

Every system starts from your workflows, your data, and your controls. We build what the engagement needs, integrate it into the stack you already run, and cut anything that exists only to look impressive in a demo.

02 /

AI trained on subject-matter expertise

Our builds are shaped by people who have negotiated the master agreements, run the diligence rooms, and carried the compliance calendars. Domain judgment is engineered into the system — not left as a disclaimer in the user guide.

03 /

A better human experience

Success is measured at the desk: fewer twelve-hour document days, faster answers with sources attached, and professionals doing the work that actually requires them. If the humans aren't better off, the system isn't done.
